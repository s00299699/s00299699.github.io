Subject Analysis:

― Food Technology ―

We need a broad knowledge of food properties, processing, preparation, nutritional considerations and consumption patterns. Hygiene both environmental and physical are placed of high importance, safe working practices and legislation in relation to the production of food as well as the effects that food has on each individuals quality of life. Food trends are constantly changing so we need to be up to date with food related issues and have practical experience to allow us to make informed and appropriate choices. We as teachers also need the knowledge and confidence to evaluate situations involving food and how to select the appropriate ingredients, methods and equipment.

―Textiles Technology―

We as teachers need the knowledge of the properties, performance and uses of textiles in todays modern context as well as the history of the textiles industry. The history, cultural uses and contemporary perspectives on textile design to create a textile product that best fits the consumers needs and wants. This will aid in the ability to provide the best support to each student when asking for assisstance. We as teachers also need the knowledge and confidence to evaluate situations involving textiles and how to select the appropriate materials, methods and equipment.

―Graphics Technology―

We need a Broad knowledge of the upcoming trends and designs. We need the knowledge of computers and the aility to deconstruct real world problems. The knowledge to convey information visually and aesthetically to convey a universal language. We as teachers also need the knowledge and confidence to evaluate situations involving graphics and how to select the appropriate materials, methods and equipment.

― Industrial Technology (Timber) ―

We need to be able to know about the current and emerging technologies in industrial and domestic settings. As well as knowing the interrelationship of technologies, equipment and materials used in a variety of settings. We also need to know Work Health and Safety (WHS) rules and regulations to ensure a safe learning environment both inside and outside the workshop. We as teachers also need the knowledge and confidence to evaluate situations involving timber and how to select the appropriate materials, methods and equipment.

―Industrial Technology (Metal)―

We need to be able to know about the current and emerging technologies in industrial and domestic settings. The importance of metal is an important learning point that should be explored to aid them in developing the neccessary skills to complete this unit of study. We also need to know Work Health and Safety (WHS) rules and regulations to ensure a safe learning environment both inside and outside the workshop. We as teachers also need the knowledge and confidence to evaluate situations involving metals and how to select the appropriate materials, methods and equipment.

―Information & Software Technology―

We need to be aware of the scope, limitations and implications of information and software technologies. Working in groups can aid the learning abilities and is seen as important when completing work. We as teachers also need the knowledge and confidence to evaluate situations involving real life situations and computers.

― Technology Mandatory (7-8) ―

Teachers need the knowledge in design and production activities to identify the needs and opportunities for a particular consumer. We need a basic understanding of the units Agriculture and Food Technologies, Digital Technologies, Engineered Systems and Material Technologies which a more detailed breakdown is seen above. We as teachers also need the knowledge and confidence to evaluate situations involving food items, textiles and computers to select the appropriate materials, methods and equipment.

―Design and Technology―

As teachers we need to be able to develop a student’s ability for innovative and creative thought. We need the knowledge of how to deal with real life situations and scenarios to provide a suitable product fit for purpose. We also need to know Work Health and Safety (WHS) rules and regulations to ensure a safe learning environment both inside and outside the workshop. We as teachers also need the knowledge and confidence to evaluate situations involving all the elements of design and production to select the appropriate materials, methods and equipment.
